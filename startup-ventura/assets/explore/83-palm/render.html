83 Palm Street · Coworking Concept

The former American Legion hall, reimagined as Startup Ventura's coworking and accelerator home: the 53' × 62' dining hall under its 25' bowstring arch, the 16' curved-front stage with new restrooms tucked behind it, kitchen café, a 16-seat board room in the old committee + check rooms, and a 21' × 26' game court in the enclosed court with its own entrance. Modeled over Kenneth H. Hess's 1948 drawings.

Why this works for the building

The 1948 bowstring trusses, arch roof and stage proscenium are untouched and become the identity of the space. The mezzanine is a freestanding steel insert and the offices are demountable glass, so the major improvements are reversible. The committee and check rooms return to service as a 16-seat board room and the already-enclosed court becomes a member game room with its own west-side entrance, so evening events can run without opening the whole floor. New restrooms tuck into the kitchen's wrap-around service zone behind the stage, keeping new plumbing in the back of house and off the historic hall. A nonprofit anchor tenant brings 7-day activation: coworking by day, accelerator programming, demo nights and community events after hours.
