The Stakes

Three numbers tell the whole story.

It’s getting too expensive to stay.

The median home hit a record $975,000 last year. A decade ago, about half of local households could afford the median home. Today it’s about one in seven.

So we’re losing people, and getting older.

The population has declined since 2017, even as California grew. The average resident is now nearly 40, up from 34 in 2000.

High-growth companies are the fix.

Widely cited research finds that every tech job creates about five more local jobs. The Trade Desk proves it can happen here. One founder. Thousands of jobs.
